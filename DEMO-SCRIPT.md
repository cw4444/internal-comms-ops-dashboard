# Demo Script

## 30-Second Version

This is a proof-of-concept internal comms operations dashboard designed for one human operator plus AI. Instead of treating internal comms as just writing, it treats it as an operational workflow: intake, prioritisation, channel planning, drafting, approvals, content reuse and insight monitoring all in one place. The prototype shows how AI can remove a lot of the repetitive coordination and drafting load while leaving judgement with the human operator.

## 60-Second Version

This prototype explores what an enterprise internal comms function could look like if it was designed for one experienced operator working with AI support.

The idea is that internal comms teams spend a lot of time on repetitive operational tasks: chasing inputs, triaging requests, rewriting the same message for multiple channels, tracking approvals and answering the same questions repeatedly. So I built a dashboard that brings that whole workflow together.

You can submit a request through a structured intake form, the system scores it for urgency, reach, risk and complexity, recommends audiences and channels, then generates a full draft pack for email, intranet, FAQ and manager brief formats. Below that, the same interface shows workflow status, the editorial calendar, reusable content assets, campaign performance and an AI insight layer that flags overlap, duplication and likely employee questions.

It is deliberately lightweight technically, but realistic from a product and workflow point of view. The point was to build something people can actually open and understand as a believable proof of concept.

## 2-Minute Guided Walkthrough

1. Start at the top of the dashboard.
   Explain that this is the operating picture for a single comms operator managing a large enterprise workflow with AI support.

2. Open the request intake section.
   Say that this standardises incoming requests and removes the chaos of ad hoc stakeholder asks.

3. Submit or describe a request.
   Call out the scoring logic: urgency, reach, risk and complexity.

4. Show the recommendation panel.
   Explain that channel choice is treated as a decision, not a default.

5. Click through the draft tabs.
   Highlight that the system generates usable first drafts for multiple internal formats instead of only a single email.

6. Move to the workflow tracker and calendar.
   Explain that delivery and approvals are part of the same system, not separate admin overhead.

7. Show the repository and search.
   Point out that reuse is operationally important because internal comms teams often recreate already-approved content.

8. Finish on analytics and AI insights.
   Explain that this is where the system becomes more than a drafting tool: it helps identify noise, collisions and likely employee concerns before they become problems.

## Interview-Friendly Framing

Because the real opportunity with AI is not just generating content faster. It is redesigning the workflow around the human so they can spend less time on admin drag and more time on judgement, sequencing and communication quality.

I would add persistence, approvals routing, export workflows, integration with collaboration channels and real analytics feeds so the prototype becomes a genuine internal operating product.
